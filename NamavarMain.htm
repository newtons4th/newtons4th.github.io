Namavar and Me!!

By Thomas J Gustafson
The Man The Machine The Apprentice

Dr. Fereydoon Namavar is a noted physicist who conducted research for the University of Nebraska Medical Center Department of Orthopedic Surgery and Rehabilitation. He specialized in a form of physical vapor deposition (PVD) known as Ion Beam Assisted Depostion (IBAD). As the primary investigator at UNMC's Nano Bio-Technology Laboratory, Dr. Namavar's research focused on the development and testing of nano thin film modification of orthopedic implants and devices. Prior to 2010 Dr. Namavar demonstrated, with the help of his amazing assistant Thomas J Gustafson, that a ceramic nano-film of zirconium dioxide had several surface characteristics beneficial to orthopedic implants. These benefits included an increase in Moh's hardness, a greather hydrophilicity to increase natural lubrication and decrease wear, and a hightened compatibility with the osteo stem cells responsible for new bone growth around an orthopedic implant. These benefits also lead to a decrease in the risk of infection after surgery.

Dr Namavar is originally from Iran. He earned his Science Doctorate in Belgium before his research career in the United States. He has recently retired and relocated to California.
